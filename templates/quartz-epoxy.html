Quartz Epoxy Flooring for South Florida's Wet & High-Traffic Environments

Quartz aggregate epoxy is the industry-standard flooring solution for environments where slip resistance, chemical exposure, and moisture are daily challenges. SPF Industrial broadcasts quartz aggregate into a high-build epoxy base coat, creating a textured, anti-slip surface that exceeds OSHA traction standards while remaining seamless, easy to clean, and highly resistant to the chemicals used in commercial kitchens, healthcare, and food processing.

Our quartz epoxy installations serve commercial kitchens, restaurant kitchens, food processing plants, healthcare facilities, pharmaceutical labs, locker rooms, pool decks, and manufacturing wash areas across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach County.

What Every SPF Quartz Epoxy Installation Includes:

  • ✓ OSHA-Compliant Slip Resistance — Quartz aggregate broadcast systems achieve a coefficient of friction of 0.6–0.8 (dry) — exceeding OSHA's 0.5 minimum for commercial walkways and meeting ADA requirements for accessible paths of travel in wet environments
  • ✓ Seamless, Coved-Base Installation — Quartz epoxy is installed as a fully seamless system with an integral coved base at wall junctions — eliminating grout lines and floor-to-wall joints where bacteria, moisture, and cleaning chemicals accumulate
  • ✓ Chemical & Moisture Resistance — The quartz-filled epoxy matrix provides excellent resistance to acids, alkalis, cleaning chemicals, fats, oils, and the moisture vapor common in South Florida's high-water-table environment — tested per ASTM C267
  • ✓ Antimicrobial Additive Options — Silver-ion antimicrobial additives are available for healthcare, pharmaceutical, and food-grade applications — providing built-in protection against bacteria, mold, and mildew within the floor system itself
  • ✓ Multiple Aggregate Sizes & Colors — Available in fine, medium, and coarse quartz aggregate sizes with 20+ color options — from neutral grays and beiges to safety yellow and tile-look patterns. Custom color chips can be blended to match your facility's existing design

Quartz Epoxy Flooring — Frequently Asked Questions

Standard smooth epoxy becomes dangerously slippery when wet — making it unsuitable for commercial kitchens, food processing areas, and wet manufacturing without added anti-slip grit. Quartz aggregate broadcast epoxy incorporates graded quartz sand directly into the surface, creating a permanently textured, anti-slip profile that exceeds OSHA traction requirements even when wet with water, oils, or cleaning solutions. The texture is built into the system — it cannot be cleaned away or worn smooth under normal commercial use.

Yes. SPF Industrial's quartz epoxy systems are installed as seamless, coved-base floors compatible with USDA, FDA, and HACCP requirements for food contact zones. The system is resistant to food-grade cleaning chemicals, sanitizers, and the mechanical cleaning required in commercial food environments. Antimicrobial silver-ion additives are available for pharmaceutical and food-grade applications. All products are compatible with NSF-listed sanitizers and cleaning protocols.

Quartz epoxy is one of the easiest commercial floors to maintain. Daily cleaning: auto-scrubber or mop with a pH-neutral cleaner — no special chemicals required. For food service environments: standard commercial degreasers and sanitizers compatible with epoxy surfaces. The textured aggregate surface can trap grease if not cleaned regularly, so daily scrubbing is recommended in high-grease commercial kitchens. Deep cleaning with a floor machine and degreaser restores the surface easily. No stripping, waxing, or re-coating is required for years.

With proper surface preparation and professional installation, a quartz aggregate epoxy floor in a South Florida commercial kitchen typically lasts 7–15 years before requiring topcoat restoration. Factors that affect longevity: daily washdown frequency, chemical exposure, heavy equipment dragging, and the quality of the original surface preparation. SPF Industrial's installation process — which includes diamond grinding, moisture vapor testing, and a high-build epoxy base coat — maximizes the system's service life in South Florida's demanding environment.

Quartz epoxy flooring in South Florida typically ranges from $6–$12 per square foot depending on the system thickness, slab condition, surface prep required, coved base specifications, aggregate color, and total square footage.
