Professional Concrete Grind & Seal Services Across South Florida

A concrete grind and seal is one of the most cost-effective ways to protect a raw or deteriorating concrete floor from South Florida's moisture vapor, surface dusting, oil staining, and chemical spills — without the cost and complexity of a full resinous coating system. SPF Industrial uses industrial diamond tooling and manufacturer-warrantied penetrating sealers from AmeriPolish, Prosoco, and L&M Construction Chemicals.

Our grind and seal services serve warehouses, distribution centers, retail spaces, garages, and light industrial facilities across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach County — from Miami and Hialeah to Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton, and West Palm Beach.

What Every SPF Grind & Seal Installation Includes:

  • ✓ Diamond Surface Profiling — We use industrial diamond tooling to remove surface laitance, open the concrete pores, and create the correct profile for sealer penetration — critical for long-term adhesion and protection
  • ✓ Shot Blasting & Contamination Removal — Existing oil, grease, adhesive residue, and surface coatings are removed before sealing — sealing over a contaminated surface guarantees early failure
  • ✓ Penetrating Sealer Application — We apply lithium silicate or densifier sealers that penetrate the concrete matrix, chemically react with calcium silicate hydrate, and harden the slab from within — producing a dust-proof, moisture-resistant surface
  • ✓ Manufacturer-Warrantied Products — SPF Industrial uses AmeriPolish, Prosoco Guard +, and L&M Cure & Seal products — not contractor-grade hardware store sealers — ensuring long-term protection with verifiable manufacturer backing
  • ✓ Final Surface Polish (Optional) — Available in matte, satin, or semi-gloss finishes depending on your facility's aesthetic and slip-resistance requirements — meets OSHA traction standards

Concrete Grind & Seal — Frequently Asked Questions

A grind and seal is a two-step process: first, diamond tooling is used to grind and profile the concrete surface, removing laitance, contamination, and surface defects. Then, a penetrating sealer (lithium silicate, densifier, or topical sealer) is applied to harden and protect the slab. It's the right solution when you want to protect or enhance a concrete floor without a full coating system — ideal for warehouses, retail spaces, garages, and light industrial facilities where moderate protection is sufficient.

Grind and seal and polished concrete both start with diamond grinding, but polished concrete uses progressively finer diamond grit (up to 3000 grit) to achieve a high-gloss, mirror-like finish that hardens the slab surface itself. Grind and seal typically uses coarser grits for profile preparation and then applies a surface or penetrating sealer for protection. Polished concrete is more durable and reflective; grind and seal is faster and more cost-effective. SPF Industrial can help you determine the right system for your budget and performance needs.

SPF Industrial uses manufacturer-warrantied products from AmeriPolish, Prosoco, and L&M Construction Chemicals — industry-leading brands used in large-scale commercial and institutional projects. These penetrating densifiers chemically react with the concrete matrix to produce a hardened, dust-proof surface. We do not use hardware store-grade sealers. The difference in product quality directly impacts how long your sealed floor performs before re-treatment.

With proper surface preparation, a professional grind and seal using penetrating lithium silicate densifiers can last 5–10 years before re-treatment under moderate traffic. Topical sealers used in retail or light commercial environments typically last 2–5 years depending on foot traffic and cleaning habits. South Florida's humidity is a factor — moisture vapor transmission must be measured and addressed during prep to prevent sealer failure. SPF Industrial performs moisture vapor emission testing on every project.

Concrete grind and seal in South Florida typically ranges from $2–$5 per square foot depending on the existing surface condition, contamination level, grind depth required, sealer type, and square footage. Larger projects benefit from economies of scale. Projects with heavy oil contamination or existing coating removal will run higher.
